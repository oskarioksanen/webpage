Projects

Master's thesis

My master's thesis compares supervised machine learning methods against traditional analytical modeling for predicting air handling unit module assembly times. Using collected production data, four ML algorithms are evaluated to determine if data-driven approaches can provide more accurate predictions than conventional process study methods.

Bachelor's thesis

My bachelor's thesis examines the applications, benefits, and challenges of applying artificial intelligence in manufacturing. The study focuses on four key areas: manufacturing cost estimation, production planning, process control, and equipment maintenance. Results demonstrate that AI can be widely applied across manufacturing to improve decision-making, product quality, resource efficiency, and equipment lifespan, providing significant competitive advantages.

Switch assembly robot

As part of my studies, I participated in a project to develop a robotic system for assembling electrical switches. In the project, we integrated a Robotiq wrist camera for vision guidance and designed 3D-printed components to support the assembly process. The project provided valuable experience in robotics, automation, and industrial applications.
